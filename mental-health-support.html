About Our Mental Health Initiative

Our Mental Health Support Program addresses the growing mental health concerns among youths in Winnipeg through a comprehensive approach that combines education, peer support, and community engagement.

"This program gave me the tools to understand my anxiety and the confidence to seek help when I need it."
Program Participant, Age 17

Core Components

Our interactive workshops cover essential mental health topics tailored for youth:

  • Understanding anxiety and depression
  • Building emotional resilience
  • Healthy coping mechanisms
  • Digital wellness and social media balance

Workshops are available in schools, community centers, and virtually, with culturally sensitive adaptations for diverse groups.

We facilitate safe, confidential peer support groups where youth can share experiences and learn from each other:

  • Age-specific groups (13-15, 16-18, 19-24)
  • LGBTQ+ affirming spaces
  • Newcomer-focused groups
  • Trained peer facilitators

Groups meet weekly with optional monthly social activities to build community connections.

Our community-wide initiatives challenge mental health stigma through:

  • Youth-created awareness campaigns
  • School presentations by lived-experience speakers
  • Social media challenges promoting mental health conversations
  • Annual "Mind Matters" community festival

Program Impact

Education Reach

Over 500 youth participate in our workshops annually, with 89% reporting improved mental health literacy.

Peer Support

120+ regular participants in our peer networks, with demonstrated reductions in isolation and improved coping skills.
