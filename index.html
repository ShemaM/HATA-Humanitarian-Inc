    About HATA Humanitarian

    At the heart of our work is a belief that every person deserves the opportunity to thrive with dignity, resilience, and purpose.

    Our Vision

    An inclusive community where every individual is empowered to thrive with resilience, dignity, and purpose.

    Our Mission

    Our mission is to empower and transform lives by promoting mental well-being, fostering inclusivity, and supporting addiction recovery through education, advocacy, and community engagement.

    Mindset Education

    Providing education in schools to build resilience

    Newcomer Support

    Helping newcomers acquire essential life skills

    Recovery Journey

    Supporting youth on their recovery journey

    Commitment to Equity

    Evidence-based approaches guided by compassion

    Leadership

    Our Board of Directors

    Our work is guided by a passionate leadership team with deep community roots and a commitment to creating change.

    Pacific Muhumure

    CEO & Director

    Pacific Muhumure is a dedicated professional with a passion for community empowerment. He is pursuing his second-degree program at the University of Manitoba and holds a degree from Southern New Hampshire University, along with certifications from Harvard University, Boston University, and the United Nations. As a former refugee who has faced immense challenges, Pacific is dedicated to sharing his journey and teaching the importance of mental health and mindset education to inspire young people to unlock their full potential.

    Angelique Nantabara

    Community Leader | Advocate

    Angelique has been a dedicated community leader and advocate in Winnipeg for over 17 years. Originally from the Democratic Republic of Congo, she brings her lived experience to her work, supporting youth, newcomers, and individuals with disabilities. Beyond Canada, Angelique has been involved in global community development, demonstrating her commitment to a compassionate and culturally informed approach.

    Gentille Ntwayingabo

    Youth Advocate | Community Builder

    Gentille Ntwayingabo immigrated to Canada from the Democratic Republic of Congo and has proudly called Winnipeg home for over 16 years. Her journey has inspired her lifelong passion for empowering others, especially youth. Currently pursuing a degree in Business Administration, Gentille brings deep lived experience and heartfelt commitment to her work.
