About Our Organization

Background

At the heart of our work is a powerful belief: that every individual, regardless of their background or circumstances, deserves the opportunity to thrive with dignity, resilience, and purpose. Founded in Winnipeg, HATA Humanitarian Inc. exists to bridge the gaps in mental health care, addiction recovery, and newcomer integration ensuring no one is left behind.

Our vision of an inclusive community where every individual is empowered to thrive drives all that we do. Through targeted programs that address mental well-being, combat stigma, and foster inclusive support systems, we aim to create lasting transformation not just for individuals, but for entire communities.

Our Mission

To empower and transform lives by promoting mental well-being, supporting addiction recovery, and facilitating social inclusion. We achieve this through education, advocacy, and strong community engagement.

Whether it's providing mindset education in schools, helping newcomers acquire life skills, or supporting youth facing addiction with food assistance and recovery mentorship, our work is guided by compassion, evidence-based approaches, and a commitment to equity.

Our Vision

An inclusive community where every individual is empowered to thrive with resilience, dignity, and purpose.

Objectives

  1. To promote mental well-being and break the stigma surrounding mental health through mindset education and support programs.
  2. To facilitate the integration of newcomers into Winnipeg by providing essential skills training and support services.
  3. To support youth struggling with addiction by offering food assistance and mindset education for long-term recovery.
  4. To build a supportive network that encourages resilience, personal growth, and social inclusion.

"We empower and transform lives by promoting mental well-being, fostering inclusivity, and supporting addiction recovery through education, advocacy, and community engagement."

Commitment to our community

Our Core Values

Dignity and Respect

We believe every person, regardless of background or circumstance, deserves to be treated with dignity, compassion, and respect. We honour the unique journey of each individual and uphold their worth in every interaction.

Inclusion and Belonging

We are committed to fostering inclusive communities where everyone newcomers, youth, marginalized individuals feels seen, valued, and supported. Diversity is our strength, and belonging is our goal.

Integrity and Accountability

We act with transparency, honesty, and a strong sense of responsibility. Our decisions and actions reflect our mission, and we are accountable to the people and communities we serve.

Empowerment through Education

Knowledge transforms lives. We provide mindset education, life skills, and training to equip individuals with the tools they need to overcome challenges and shape their futures.

Hope and Transformation

We believe in the power of hope to ignite change. From addiction recovery to newcomer integration, we walk alongside individuals on their journey to transformation and purpose.

Community-Centered Action

Our work is grounded in the lived experiences of the communities we serve. We engage collaboratively, build trust, and co-create solutions that bring lasting change.

Our Leadership Team

Pacific Muhumure

CEO & Director

Pacific is a dedicated professional with degrees in Healthcare Management and certifications from Harvard University and the UN. He brings extensive experience in education and community empowerment.

Passionate about community empowerment, Pacific has collaborated with NGOs, government agencies, and the Ministry of Education to serve underserved communities. His diverse experience includes roles as Academic Advisor, Monitoring and Evaluation Officer, and Project Manager.

Having faced challenges as a young refugee, Pacific is committed to encouraging students by sharing his journey and teaching the importance of mental health and mindset education.

Angelique Nantabara

Community Leader | Advocate

Angelique has been a dedicated community advocate in Winnipeg for over 17 years, bringing deep passion and lived experience to her work supporting youth, newcomers, and individuals with disabilities.

Originally from the Democratic Republic of Congo, she immigrated to Canada and quickly became an active force in settlement services and community integration. Beyond Canada, she has spent more than a decade supporting forcibly displaced families in Congo.

Angelique served as the overall leader of Ineza Organization, advocating for inclusion and support systems for newcomers and displaced families.

Gentile Ntwayingabo

Youth Advocate | Community Builder

Gentile immigrated to Canada from the Democratic Republic of Congo as a young girl and has proudly called Winnipeg home for over 16 years. She is passionate about empowering youth and newcomers.

A graduate of Golden Bell High School, Gentile is currently pursuing a Bachelor of Arts degree in Business Administration. She has worked extensively in community development, supporting youth, students, and individuals with disabilities.

Gentile understands firsthand the barriers young immigrants face and dedicates herself to helping others overcome those hurdles to realize their full potential.
